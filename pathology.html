Pathology Vocabulary

Learn key pathology terms used in diseases, diagnosis, and laboratory medicine.

Term Definition
AbscessA localized collection of pus due to infection.
AnemiaCondition marked by low red blood cells or hemoglobin.
ApoptosisProgrammed cell death, a normal process in tissue development.
AtrophyDecrease in size or wasting away of a tissue or organ.
CarcinomaMalignant tumor arising from epithelial cells.
CystClosed sac-like structure filled with fluid or semi-solid material.
EdemaExcess fluid accumulation in tissues, causing swelling.
FibrosisFormation of excess fibrous connective tissue as a reparative response.
HypertrophyIncrease in size of an organ due to cell enlargement.
InflammationBody’s response to injury or infection (redness, heat, swelling, pain).
IschemiaReduced blood flow to tissue, causing oxygen deprivation.
LeukemiaCancer of blood-forming tissues.
MetaplasiaReversible change where one cell type is replaced by another.
NecrosisPremature death of cells or tissue due to injury or disease.
NeoplasmAbnormal growth of tissue, benign or malignant.
PathogenMicroorganism that causes disease.
SepsisLife-threatening organ dysfunction caused by infection.
ThrombosisFormation of a blood clot inside a blood vessel.
TumorAn abnormal mass of tissue that may be benign or malignant.
UlcerOpen sore on skin or mucous membrane due to tissue breakdown.
LymphomaA type of blood cancer that develops in the lymphatic system.
CytologyThe study of cells, especially for the diagnosis of diseases.
HistopathologyThe microscopic examination of tissue to study the manifestations of disease.
GranulomaA small area of inflammation caused by infection or foreign substances.
SarcomaA malignant tumor of connective or supportive tissue.
MetastasisThe spread of cancer cells from the original site to other parts of the body.
AtherosclerosisHardening and narrowing of the arteries due to plaque buildup.
HemorrhageExcessive bleeding, either internal or external.
JaundiceYellowing of the skin and eyes due to liver dysfunction or bile obstruction.
CirrhosisChronic liver damage causing scarring and liver failure.
EmbolismObstruction of a blood vessel by a blood clot, air bubble, or fat droplet.
GangreneDeath of body tissue due to lack of blood supply or severe infection.
LesionA region in an organ or tissue that has suffered damage due to injury or disease.
BiopsyRemoval of tissue for diagnostic examination.
SequelaeConditions that are the consequence of a previous disease or injury.
EtiologyThe study of the cause or origin of a disease.
PrognosisThe likely course or outcome of a disease.
DiagnosisIdentification of the nature of an illness or condition.
RemissionDecrease or disappearance of symptoms of disease.
RelapseThe return of disease after partial recovery.
ChronicA disease that is long-lasting or recurrent.
AcuteA disease with rapid onset and/or short course.
SepticemiaA serious bloodstream infection that can lead to sepsis.
HemolysisDestruction of red blood cells releasing hemoglobin into plasma.
HyperplasiaIncrease in the number of cells in an organ or tissue.
DysplasiaAbnormal growth or development of tissue or cells.
Myocardial InfarctionHeart attack caused by blockage of blood flow to the heart.
StrokeDamage to the brain from interruption of blood supply.
PericarditisInflammation of the pericardium, the sac surrounding the heart.
EndocarditisInflammation of the inner lining of the heart chambers and valves.
PneumothoraxCollapsed lung due to air in the pleural space.
HematemesisVomiting of blood, usually from the gastrointestinal tract.
MelenaBlack, tarry stools indicative of gastrointestinal bleeding.
PolypAn abnormal growth of tissue projecting from a mucous membrane.
BronchiectasisPermanent dilation of parts of the airways of the lung.
CholelithiasisPresence of gallstones in the gallbladder.
NephrolithiasisFormation of kidney stones in the urinary tract.
HyperthyroidismOveractive thyroid gland producing excessive thyroid hormones.
HypothyroidismUnderactive thyroid gland producing insufficient thyroid hormones.
