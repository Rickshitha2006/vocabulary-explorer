Business Vocabulary

Enhance your professional communication with essential business terms used in corporate environments.

💰 Financial Terms

Word Meaning Example
Revenue Total income generated by a company. "Revenue increased by 20% last quarter."
Profit Margin The percentage of revenue that is profit. "The company improved its profit margin this year."
ROI Return on Investment; profitability of an investment. "We need to calculate ROI before the new project."
Balance Sheet A financial statement showing assets, liabilities, and equity. "Investors reviewed the balance sheet before investing."
Cash Flow The movement of money in and out of a business. "Poor cash flow management can hurt operations."
Equity Ownership interest in a company. "The shareholders increased their equity stake."
Dividend Profit distributed to shareholders. "The company announced a 5% dividend this year."
Capital Expenditure (CapEx) Money spent to acquire or upgrade physical assets. "The factory renovation is a major CapEx."
Accounts Receivable Money owed to a company by its customers. "We need to reduce accounts receivable to improve cash flow."
Debt Financing Raising money by borrowing. "The startup chose debt financing to fund expansion."
Liquidity Availability of cash or assets easily convertible to cash. "Maintaining liquidity is essential for operations."
Gross Margin Revenue minus cost of goods sold. "The gross margin improved due to lower material costs."
Amortization Gradual repayment of debt or spreading cost over time. "Amortization of the loan will take 5 years."
Budget Variance Difference between planned and actual expenses. "The budget variance must be explained to management."
Hedge Strategy to reduce financial risk. "The company used futures contracts to hedge against currency risk."
Accounts Payable Money a company owes to suppliers. "Accounts payable must be cleared before month-end."
Accrual Recording revenues and expenses when they are incurred, not when cash is exchanged. "Accrual accounting provides a more accurate picture of performance."
Capital Gains Profit from selling an asset at a higher price than its purchase price. "Investors earned significant capital gains from stocks."
Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) Direct costs of producing goods sold by a company. "COGS decreased due to cheaper raw materials."
Depreciation Reduction in value of assets over time. "Depreciation of machinery is accounted annually."
Equity Financing Raising money by selling shares of a company. "Equity financing helped the startup expand."
Financial Ratios Metrics used to evaluate a company's performance (e.g., debt-to-equity). "Liquidity ratios indicate how easily the company can pay short-term obligations."
Inflation General increase in prices, reducing purchasing power. "Inflation affects interest rates and investment returns."
Leverage Ratio Measurement of debt compared to equity or assets. "A high leverage ratio may indicate financial risk."
Net Income Profit after all expenses and taxes are deducted. "The company reported a net income of $2M."
Operating Expenses (OPEX) Costs required for daily operations. "Reducing OPEX improved overall profitability."
Portfolio Diversification Spreading investments to reduce risk. "Portfolio diversification protects against market volatility."
Reserves Funds set aside for future obligations or emergencies. "The company created reserves for unexpected expenses."
Shareholder Equity Owner’s claim after liabilities are deducted. "Shareholder equity increased due to retained earnings."
Venture Capital Investment in early-stage startups with high growth potential. "The startup received venture capital funding for expansion."
Working Capital Ratio Current assets divided by current liabilities. "A ratio above 1 indicates good liquidity."
Bond Debt security where an investor loans money to a company or government. "The company issued bonds to fund its new project."
Derivative Financial contract whose value depends on an underlying asset. "Derivatives are used for hedging risks."
Hedging Using financial instruments to reduce potential losses. "The company hedged its currency exposure."
Contingent Liability Potential liability dependent on a future event. "Pending lawsuits are considered contingent liabilities."

📈 Marketing Terms

Word Meaning Example

Target Audience Specific group of consumers a company aims to reach. "The ad campaign focused on a young target audience."
Brand Equity The value a brand adds to a product or service. "Strong brand equity helps increase customer loyalty."
Value Proposition The benefit a product or service offers customers. "Our value proposition is affordable quality."
Churn Rate The percentage of customers who stop using a service over time. "Reducing churn rate is key for SaaS companies."
Brand Awareness How familiar consumers are with a brand. "Advertising campaigns increased brand awareness."
Conversion Rate Percentage of users completing a desired action. "The website redesign improved conversion rate by 10%."
Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Cost to acquire a new customer. "Reducing CAC is vital for profitability."
Engagement Rate Measure of interaction with content. "The post had an engagement rate of 15%."
Lead Generation Process of attracting potential customers. "The marketing team focused on lead generation through social media."
Retargeting Advertising to users who previously engaged. "Retargeting campaigns increased sales conversions."
Brand Loyalty Customer commitment to a brand. "Brand loyalty ensures repeat purchases."
Market Segmentation Dividing market into distinct groups. "Market segmentation allows targeted campaigns."
Viral Marketing Promotion that spreads quickly through sharing. "The video became a viral marketing hit."
Influencer Marketing Using influential people to promote products. "The brand invested in influencer marketing for product launch."
Conversion Rate The percentage of users who take a desired action (e.g., buy, sign up). "Our website's conversion rate improved after the redesign."
Brand Awareness The extent to which consumers recognize a brand. "Social media campaigns increased brand awareness."
Customer Acquisition The process of gaining new customers. "Marketing strategies focused on customer acquisition this quarter."
Customer Retention The ability to keep existing customers over time. "Loyalty programs help improve customer retention."
Engagement Rate Measure of how users interact with content or campaigns. "The engagement rate on Instagram posts rose to 8%."
Call to Action (CTA) A prompt that encourages users to take action. "The landing page had a clear call to action to subscribe."
Lead Generation The process of attracting potential customers for a product or service. "The campaign was successful in lead generation."
Market Segmentation Dividing a target market into distinct groups with common needs. "Market segmentation helps create personalized campaigns."
Positioning How a brand or product is perceived in the mind of the customer. "The company focused on premium positioning for its new product."
Remarketing Targeting ads to users who have previously interacted with your brand. "Remarketing helped recover abandoned shopping carts."
Brand Loyalty Customers’ consistent preference for a specific brand. "High brand loyalty reduces marketing costs over time."
Customer Journey The complete experience a customer has with a brand from awareness to purchase. "Understanding the customer journey improved sales conversion."
Omnichannel Marketing Integrated marketing across multiple channels (online/offline). "Omnichannel marketing improved customer engagement."
Viral Marketing Marketing that spreads rapidly via social media or word-of-mouth. "The campaign went viral, reaching millions of viewers."
Affiliate Marketing Promoting products via partners and earning a commission. "The company increased sales through affiliate marketing."
Content Marketing Creating and sharing valuable content to attract customers. "Blogs and videos are used in content marketing."
Influencer Marketing Collaborating with influencers to promote products. "Influencer marketing boosted brand visibility."
Public Relations (PR) Managing the public image and communication of a company. "Effective PR improved the company’s reputation."
Search Engine Optimization (SEO) Optimizing online content to rank higher in search engines. "SEO efforts increased organic website traffic."
Click-Through Rate (CTR) The percentage of users who click on an ad or link. "The CTR for the ad campaign was 4%."
